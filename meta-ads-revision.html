Topic 01
What is Meta Advertising?

Meta Advertising enables businesses to reach highly targeted audiences across Meta's family of apps — Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, Threads, and the Audience Network — using a single unified platform.

At its core, Meta's system operates on a real-time auction model where ads compete based on bid amount, ad quality, and relevance to each user. The platform serves over 3 billion monthly active users, making it one of the highest-reach ad platforms on Earth.

2025 Shift: Meta is moving rapidly toward full AI automation. Manual targeting controls are shrinking. The platform now favours broad targeting + strong creatives + clean data signals — letting the algorithm do the heavy lifting.

The Meta Ad Auction — 3 Key Factors

  • Bid Amount — What you're willing to pay per result (click, conversion, view)
  • Ad Quality & Relevance — How well your ad resonates with the target audience (measured by engagement signals)
  • Estimated Action Rates — How likely the user is to take the action you're optimising for
Key Formula: Total Value = Bid × Estimated Action Rate × Ad Quality Score. The highest total value wins the auction — not just the highest bid.

Topic 02
Campaign Objectives — ODAX Framework

Meta uses the ODAX (Outcome-Driven Ad Experiences) framework — fully rolled out in 2022 and now the standard. There are 6 campaign objectives, each telling Meta's algorithm what outcome to optimise for. Choosing the wrong objective is one of the biggest mistakes advertisers make.

⚠️ Critical: If you choose "Traffic" but want "Purchases", Meta's algorithm will find users likely to click — not users likely to buy. Always match your objective to your actual business goal.
ObjectiveWhat Meta Optimises ForBest Use CaseFunnel Stage
🔔 AwarenessReach, impressions, video views, brand recallNew brand/product launches, broad reach campaignsTop
🖱️ TrafficLink clicks, landing page viewsDriving visitors to website or appTop/Mid
💬 EngagementLikes, comments, shares, page follows, event responsesBuilding social proof, growing communityTop/Mid
📋 LeadsForm fills (Meta Instant Forms or website leads)B2B, services, finance, educationMid/Bottom
📱 App PromotionApp installs, in-app events, re-engagementMobile apps wanting installs or activityBottom
🛒 SalesPurchases, add-to-cart, conversions with valueE-commerce, direct response, ROAS campaignsBottom
2025 Best Performers: Sales and Leads objectives consistently deliver the highest ROI for performance marketers. Use Awareness only when brand reach is the explicit goal.

Topic 03
Campaign Structure
Campaign — Set your objective (Awareness, Traffic, Leads, Sales…). Also set Campaign Budget Optimisation (CBO) here.
Ad Set — Define targeting, placements, budget (if not using CBO), schedule, and optimisation event.
Ad — The creative: image, video, carousel, copy, CTA, and destination URL.

Budget Types

Daily Budget
Consistent spend per day. Good for always-on campaigns. Meta may spend up to 25% more on high-opportunity days.
Lifetime Budget
Total spend over the campaign period. Meta distributes it based on opportunity — useful for time-limited campaigns.
CBO (Campaign Budget Optimisation): Set the budget at Campaign level and let Meta distribute it across Ad Sets automatically in real time. More efficient than manual ad-set budgets. Now the default in Advantage+ campaigns.
Learning Phase: Every new ad set enters a Learning Phase where Meta's algorithm gathers data. You need ~50 optimisation events per week to exit it. Avoid editing campaigns during this phase — changes reset the learning clock.

Topic 05
Audience Targeting
2025 Trend: Meta is eliminating granular targeting controls. Many detailed interest options have been removed. The winning strategy is now Go Broad → Feed Clean Data → Let AI Optimise.

Audience Types

Audience TypeHow It WorksBest For
Core AudiencesManual targeting by demographics, location, interests, and behavioursTesting, small budgets, niche markets
Custom AudiencesBuilt from your own data: website visitors (Pixel), customer lists (email/phone), app activity, video viewers, Instagram engagersRetargeting, warm audiences, Customer Match
Lookalike AudiencesMeta finds users similar to your Custom Audience. 1% = tightest match; 10% = broadest. Use Advantage+ Lookalike for dynamic expansion.Prospecting, scaling proven audiences
Advantage+ AudienceMeta's AI-driven audience. You optionally provide "audience suggestions", but Meta expands freely based on behavioral signals2025 best practice — most campaigns

Custom Audience Sources

  • Website Visitors — Via Meta Pixel: all visitors, product page viewers, cart abandoners, purchasers
  • Customer List — Upload email/phone; Meta matches to user accounts (similar to Google Customer Match)
  • App Activity — Users who took specific in-app actions
  • Video Engagers — People who watched 3s, 10s, 25%, 50%, 75%, or 95% of your videos
  • Lead Form Openers/Completers — Users who interacted with your Instant Forms
  • Instagram/Facebook Engagers — Users who visited your profile, liked posts, sent DMs
Retargeting in 2025: Build engagement-based retargeting from video viewers (≥3 seconds or ≥75% viewed) and page visitors. These are reliable signals, far less affected by iOS privacy restrictions than Pixel-only audiences.

Topic 06
Ad Formats
🖼️ Image Ad
Single static image. Versatile and quick to produce. Best for simple, clear messages with a strong visual. Use 1:1 for Feed, 9:16 for Stories/Reels.
▶️ Video Ad Dominant
Single video. Hook viewers in the first 3 seconds. Design for sound-off (use captions). Short-form, vertical Reels-style videos perform best in 2025.
🎠 Carousel Ad
2–10 scrollable image/video cards, each with its own link. Great for showcasing multiple products, features, or steps of a story.
📦 Collection Ad
Hero image/video + product grid below. Opens into an Instant Experience (full-screen). Best for e-commerce product discovery on mobile.
⚡ Instant Experience
Full-screen, mobile-only landing page within Meta. Loads 15x faster than external pages. Combine with Collection or Carousel ads.
📋 Lead Ad
Instant Form opens within Meta — no landing page needed. Pre-fills user data. Great for reducing friction in lead gen campaigns.
🛒 Dynamic Product Ad E-commerce
Auto-shows products from your catalog that a user viewed or is likely to buy. Requires a product feed. Powers retargeting and prospecting at scale.
🎮 Playable Ad
Interactive mini-demo of an app. Boosts install rates by 2–3x vs. static ads. Available for App Promotion campaigns.
Aspect Ratio Guide: Feed = 1:1 or 4:5 | Stories & Reels = 9:16 | Right Column = 1.91:1. Always design mobile-first — 95%+ of Meta users are on smartphones. Use Advantage+ Creative to auto-adapt assets across placements.

Topic 08
Bidding Strategies

Three Categories of Bidding

StrategyCategoryHow It WorksBest For
Highest Volume
(formerly Lowest Cost)		Spend-BasedSpends your full budget to get the maximum number of results. No cost constraints.Beginners, awareness, lead gen at scale, testing
Highest ValueSpend-BasedFocuses on conversions with the highest purchase value — not just quantity. Maximises revenue, not volume.E-commerce with varying product prices
Cost CapGoal-BasedKeeps average cost per result around your set cap. More delivery flexibility than Bid Cap.Lead gen, performance campaigns with cost targets
ROAS GoalGoal-BasedYou set a return on ad spend target (e.g., 3x). Meta aims to hit that return while spending budget.E-commerce with clear revenue targets
Bid CapManualSets a maximum bid per auction. Full control but can severely limit delivery if set too low.Advanced advertisers only — requires monitoring
Start with Highest Volume when launching new campaigns — it maximises delivery and speeds up the learning phase. Switch to Cost Cap or ROAS Goal once you have 50+ conversions/week and understand your benchmarks.

Scaling Rules

  • Scale budgets by max 20–50% per week to avoid resetting the learning phase
  • Run campaigns for at least 7 days before evaluating performance
  • Avoid frequent edits — each significant change resets the learning phase
  • Exit the learning phase by generating 50+ optimisation events per ad set per week

Topic 09
Meta Pixel & Conversions API (CAPI)

Tracking is the foundation of Meta Ads performance. Without accurate conversion data, Meta's algorithm cannot optimise, your bidding is blind, and your costs rise. In 2025, browser-based tracking is increasingly unreliable due to iOS restrictions, ad blockers, and privacy laws.

🔷 Meta Pixel
A JavaScript snippet placed on your website. Tracks page views, add-to-carts, purchases, and custom events via the browser. Affected by ad blockers and iOS privacy (App Tracking Transparency).
🔶 Conversions API (CAPI)
Server-side tracking. Sends events directly from your server to Meta — bypassing browser restrictions. More reliable, especially post-iOS 14. Use alongside Pixel with deduplication.
2025 Best Practice — Use Both Together: Pixel + CAPI with deduplication gives the most complete picture. CAPI recovers lost browser events. Even with both, ~20% of data can be lost due to cross-device tracking limitations.

Standard Events to Track

Event Match Quality (EMQ): Meta scores how well your conversion events match to real users (Good / Great / Poor). Higher EMQ = better attribution, lower CPA, stronger optimisation. Improve EMQ by passing hashed email and phone data with your conversion events.

Measurement & Key Metrics
MetricFormulaWhat to Watch
CTR (Click-Through Rate)Clicks / Impressions × 100Low CTR = weak creative or wrong audience
CPC (Cost Per Click)Spend / ClicksHigh CPC = auction competition or low relevance
CPM (Cost Per 1,000 Impressions)Spend / Impressions × 1000Rising CPM = audience too small or high competition
CPA (Cost Per Action)Spend / ConversionsCore efficiency metric — compare to target CPA
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)Revenue / Ad SpendBelow 1 = losing money; target varies by margin
FrequencyImpressions / ReachAbove 3–4 = ad fatigue risk; refresh creatives
Conversion RateConversions / Clicks × 100Low CVR = landing page or offer problem
MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio)Total Revenue / Total Ad SpendFull-picture view across all channels, not just Meta

Attribution Windows

  • 1-day click — Conversion within 1 day of clicking the ad
  • 7-day click — Conversion within 7 days of clicking (default, most common)
  • 1-day view — Conversion within 1 day of viewing (without clicking)
  • 7-day click + 1-day view — Broadest window; captures view-through conversions
Recommended: Use 7-day click as your standard attribution window. For brand awareness + remarketing funnels, add 1-day view to capture the full impact. Note: Meta's reported conversions will often differ from your backend (Shopify, CRM) — this is normal due to attribution model differences.

Topic 11
Creative Strategy — The New Targeting
"Creative is the new targeting." As Meta eliminates granular audience controls, your creative IS how you reach the right person. The algorithm uses signals from who engages with your ad to find more people like them. Better creative = better algorithmic targeting.

Creative Best Practices — 2025

  • Hook in 3 seconds — For video, the first 3 seconds determine if the user stops scrolling. Bold visuals, movement, or a surprising statement.
  • Design for sound-off — 85%+ of Meta videos are watched without sound. Use captions and text overlays.
  • Mobile-first, vertical format — 9:16 for Stories/Reels, 4:5 for Feed. Never use horizontal video as primary.
  • Native feel — Ads that look like organic content (UGC, talking-head videos, lo-fi) outperform polished "ad-like" content in most niches.
  • UGC & Social Proof — Reviews, testimonials, and user-generated content consistently outperform brand-produced content in CTR and CVR.
  • Benefit-focused copy — Lead with the benefit to the user, not the features of the product.
  • Strong CTA — Be specific: "Shop Now", "Get Free Quote", "Download the App" — not generic "Learn More."
  • Test 4–10 creatives per ad set — Let the algorithm identify winners. Rotate new creatives every 30–45 days to prevent ad fatigue.

Full-Funnel Creative Approach

1
Awareness (Cold Audience)
Introduce brand — don't sell yet
Short-form video (Reels/Stories). Brand storytelling, problem identification, educational content. UGC-style or founder story. Target: Advantage+ Audience, Lookalikes, Broad Interest.
2
Consideration (Warm Audience)
Build trust and desire
Carousel showing product features, video testimonials, how-to demos, comparison content. Target: Video viewers (25%+), Page engagers, Website visitors.
3
Conversion (Hot Audience)
Drive the purchase/lead
Dynamic Product Ads (show exact viewed products), Lead Forms with low-friction offer, strong discount or urgency-driven copy. Target: Cart abandoners, Product page viewers, Custom audiences from CRM.
4
Retention (Existing Customers)
Repeat purchase, upsell, loyalty
Customer Match with exclusive offers. Cross-sell/upsell creatives featuring complementary products. Loyalty or referral program ads. Target: Purchaser custom audiences.
Ad Fatigue Signal: Monitor Frequency — when it exceeds 3–4, engagement drops and CPMs rise. Refresh creatives proactively before fatigue sets in. Track Hook Rate (% who watch 3s+) and Hold Rate (% who finish) for video.
