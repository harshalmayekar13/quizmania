Topic 01
What is Meta Advertising?

Meta Advertising enables businesses to reach highly targeted audiences across Meta's family of apps — Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, Threads, and the Audience Network — using a single unified platform called Meta Ads Manager.

At its core, Meta's system operates on a real-time auction model where ads compete based on bid, ad quality, and estimated action rates. The platform serves over 3.3 billion monthly active users making it one of the highest-reach ad platforms globally.

2025 Fundamental Shift: Meta is moving to full AI automation. Manual targeting is shrinking. The winning strategy is now: Broad Targeting + Diverse Creatives + Clean Data Signals — let the algorithm work.

The Meta Ad Auction — 3 Key Factors

  • Bid Amount — What you're willing to pay per result
  • Estimated Action Rate — How likely the specific user is to take the desired action
  • Ad Quality & Relevance — How well your ad resonates (measured by engagement signals)
Total Value = Bid × Estimated Action Rate × Ad Quality. Highest total value wins — not just highest bid.

Campaign Structure
Campaign — Set objective + Advantage+ Budget (CBO). In 2025, Advantage+ is the default path for all campaign creation.
Ad Set — Targeting, placements, schedule, optimisation event. Advantage+ Sales now supports multiple ad sets (each up to 50 ads).
Ad — Creative: image, video, carousel, copy, CTA, destination URL.
Daily Budget
Consistent daily spend. Meta can spend up to 25% more on high-opportunity days. Good for always-on campaigns.
Lifetime Budget
Total spend over the campaign period. Meta distributes it based on opportunity. Best for time-limited promotions.
Advantage+ Campaign Budget (CBO): Set budget at Campaign level; Meta distributes it across Ad Sets automatically. Now renamed Advantage+ Budget in 2025 (Meta unified all "Advantage" naming to "Advantage+").
Learning Phase (2025 Update): Requires ~50 optimisation events per week to exit. In 2025, Meta updated guidance — adding a new ad no longer always resets the learning phase. However, significant budget changes, audience edits, or bidding strategy changes still do. Meta now shows specific messages like "You can increase budget by ₹X without restarting learning."
Opportunity Score (New in 2025): A score from 0–100 evaluating your campaign health — creative variety, signal quality, audience breadth, conversion setup. Available in Advantage+ Campaign Setup. Use it to identify issues before launching.

Topic 05
Andromeda — Meta's New Ad Engine

Andromeda is Meta's new AI-powered ad retrieval engine — the first stage of ad delivery that filters millions of candidate ads down to the most relevant few thousand for each impression. It replaced the old system between late 2024 and October 2025 (global rollout complete).

The old system couldn't handle the explosion of ad variations from AI tools. Andromeda uses ML models 10,000x larger than the previous system, scanning billions of ads per second.

Andromeda's Impact: +8% improvement in ad quality scores, +6% improvement in ad recall. 4x more efficient at driving performance gains per unit of compute vs. the old system.

How the New Delivery Pipeline Works

1
Retrieval — Andromeda
Filters millions → thousands of candidate ads
Andromeda scans all eligible ads and selects a shortlist based on predicted resonance with each user. It rewards creative diversity — if all your ads are similar, it treats them as one option. Needs variety to personalise.
2
Ranking — Lattice
Predicts likelihood of user action
Ranks Andromeda's shortlist by predicting how likely each user is to click, convert, or engage. Considers ad quality, relevance score, and bid amount.
3
Auction
Best ranked ads compete
Winning ads are selected based on Total Value (Bid × Action Rate × Quality). Because Andromeda already filtered for relevance, the auction is more efficient end-to-end.
The Andromeda Mindset Shift: Meta's March 2025 update stated: "With AI-enabled advertising tools, the focus has shifted from niche targeting to creative diversification as the best lever to find relevant audiences." Your job is now to feed the system diverse creative, not control targeting.

What Andromeda Means for Your Campaigns

  • Remove the 6-ad limit — Meta removed this guidance. Run 10–20 genuinely different creatives per ad set (not variations of the same concept)
  • Go broad on targeting — Age/gender/country only. Let AI find the buyers, not your interest stacks
  • Simplify structure — 1–3 ad sets per campaign max. Complex multi-adset structures hurt Andromeda's learning
  • Creative diversity is everything — Different hooks, formats, angles, problems addressed. Static, video, carousel — all different
  • Andromeda evaluates hooks — First 3 seconds of video are scored separately. Strong openings get prioritised

  • Meta now provides specific guidance on budget increases — check the "without restarting learning" message in Ads Manager

Topic 10
Meta Pixel & Conversions API (CAPI)

In 2025, data signal quality is the single biggest lever for Meta Ads performance. Andromeda and Advantage+ are only as good as the data you feed them. Without strong signals, the algorithm can't learn who your buyers are.

🔷 Meta Pixel
JavaScript snippet on your website. Tracks page views, add-to-carts, purchases via browser. Affected by iOS ATT, ad blockers, and cookie restrictions. Increasingly unreliable alone.
🔶 Conversions API (CAPI)
Server-side tracking — sends events directly from your server to Meta, bypassing browser restrictions. More reliable post-iOS. Essential in 2025. Use with Pixel + deduplication.
2025 Best Practice — Pixel + CAPI Together: CAPI recovers lost browser events. Sending hashed customer data (email, phone) with events improves Event Match Quality (EMQ) — Meta's score of how well events match real users. Higher EMQ = lower CPA, better optimisation, stronger Andromeda performance.

Attribution Models & Windows

Attribution tells you which ad interaction gets credit for a conversion. In Meta's ecosystem, a user might see a Reels ad, then a Feed ad, then click a Story before buying. Attribution determines which touchpoint gets the credit — and this directly affects how you evaluate performance and allocate budget.

Attribution Windows

WindowWhat It CountsBest For
1-Day ClickConversion within 1 day of clicking the adImpulse purchases, fast funnels
7-Day Click ⭐Conversion within 7 days of clickingDefault — recommended for most campaigns
1-Day View (Engaged-View)Conversion within 1 day of viewing 5+ seconds of video (updated from 10s in 2025)Video campaigns, brand awareness
7-Day Click + 1-Day ViewBroadest window — captures both click and view-throughFull-funnel campaigns, upper + lower funnel combined
2025 Update — Engaged-View threshold changed: View-through attribution now triggers at 5 seconds of video watched (down from 10 seconds). This means more conversions are attributed to video ads. Meta calls this "engaged-view attribution." Be aware this can inflate reported conversions from video campaigns.

Attribution Models

ModelHow Credit is AssignedLimitation
Last Click100% credit to the last ad click before conversionIgnores all upper-funnel touchpoints that introduced the user
Last Touch100% credit to the last interaction (click or view)Over-credits closing touchpoint; undervalues awareness
LinearEqual credit distributed across all touchpointsDoesn't distinguish high vs. low value interactions
Time DecayMore credit to touchpoints closer to the conversionUndervalues early awareness-stage ads
Data-Driven ⭐ML assigns credit based on true contribution of each touchpointRequires sufficient conversion volume to work
Meta's Default: Meta Ads Manager uses Last Touch attribution by default (7-day click + 1-day view). This means the last ad a user interacted with gets 100% of the credit. This often over-credits retargeting ads and under-credits top-of-funnel campaigns that introduced the user.

Incremental Attribution — The 2025 Gold Standard

Standard attribution (Last Touch, Last Click) tells you who converted after seeing an ad. Incremental attribution tells you how many conversions happened because of the ad — the true causal lift.

  • Conversion Lift Study — Meta splits your audience into exposed vs. holdout groups. Measures incremental conversions. Most accurate way to prove true ad impact
  • Brand Lift Study — Measures incremental brand awareness, ad recall, and consideration among exposed vs. unexposed users via surveys
  • Meta Attribution — Cross-channel measurement tool. Compares Meta-reported conversions vs. true incremental lift across your full marketing mix
Why This Matters: Meta's Ads Manager will almost always show more conversions than your Shopify/CRM backend. This is normal — Meta uses a broader attribution window and counts view-throughs. For true performance, run a Conversion Lift Study quarterly and compare against backend data. The gap between the two is your "over-attribution" — typically 20–40%.
Attribution vs. Your Backend Data:
Meta Ads Manager might report 200 purchases. Your Shopify reports 140. Both are correct — they measure differently.
• Meta counts everyone who saw/clicked your ad within the window AND converted
• Shopify counts direct last-click conversions
Truth is somewhere in the middle. Use Conversion Lift to find it.

Topic 13
Creative Strategy — The #1 Performance Lever
"Creative is the new targeting." With Andromeda and shrinking manual controls, creative diversity is now the primary way you reach the right person. The algorithm uses engagement signals from your ad to find more users like those who responded. Better creative = better algorithmic targeting.

2025 Creative Best Practices

  • Hook in 3 seconds — Andromeda scores first 3s separately. Bold movement, surprising statement, or direct benefit in the opening frame
  • Design for sound-off — 85%+ of Meta videos watched without sound. Use captions and bold text overlays
  • Mobile-first, vertical (9:16) — For Stories/Reels. Never use horizontal video as primary format
  • Native feel over polished ads — UGC, talking-head videos, lo-fi content consistently outperforms production-heavy ads in CTR and CVR
  • Run 10–20 genuinely different creatives — Not variations of the same idea. Different concepts, hooks, angles, formats. Feed Andromeda variety
  • Don't run 30–50 ads — With limited daily budget, too many ads means each gets insufficient learning data. 10–20 is the sweet spot
  • Refresh every 2–4 weeks — Monitor Frequency (fatigue above 3–4) and Hook Rate (below 25% = weak opening)
  • Use Advantage+ Creative — Let Meta generate background variations, text overlays, animated banners from your assets. 11% higher CTR on average

Full-Funnel Creative Approach

1
Awareness (Cold)
Introduce — don't sell yet
Short-form Reels/Stories video. Brand story, problem identification, educational content. UGC-style or founder story. Target: Advantage+ Audience (Broad), Lookalikes.
2
Consideration (Warm)
Build trust and desire
Carousel showing product features, video testimonials, how-to demos, comparisons. Target: Video viewers (25%+), Page engagers, Website visitors.
3
Conversion (Hot)
Drive the purchase/lead
Dynamic Product Ads (exact products viewed), strong discount/urgency copy, Lead Forms with low-friction offer. Target: Cart abandoners, Product viewers, CRM custom audiences.
4
Retention (Customers)
Repeat purchase, upsell
Customer Match with exclusive offers. Cross-sell/upsell creative. Loyalty program ads. Target: Purchaser custom audiences (exclude from prospecting campaigns).
Creative Fatigue Signals to Monitor: Frequency above 3–4 | Hook Rate below 25% | CTR dropping week-over-week | CPM rising without audience size reduction. Andromeda now proactively reduces delivery to fatigued audiences before performance drops — but you should refresh before it gets there.
