Topic 01
The Foundation: What is Google Ads?

Google Ads is an online advertising platform where businesses bid to display ads to users at the exact moment they are searching for products or services. It primarily operates on a Pay-Per-Click (PPC) model — you only pay when someone clicks your ad.

The Ad Auction: Every time a user searches, a real-time auction is triggered. The winner is determined by Ad Rank = Bid × Quality Score + Ad Extension Impact.

Quality Score is based on:

  • Expected CTR — How likely your ad is to be clicked
  • Ad Relevance — How well your ad matches the user's search intent
  • Landing Page Experience — How relevant, useful, and fast your landing page is

Account Structure Hierarchy

Account — Unique login, billing, and settings
Campaign — Budget, targeting, bidding strategy
Ad Group — Themed set of keywords and ads
Keywords — Trigger words for your ads
Ads — The creative users see
MCC (Manager Account / My Client Center): A master account that lets you manage multiple Google Ads accounts from a single dashboard. Essential for agencies.

Topic 02
Campaign Types
🔍 Search Most Common
Text ads on Google Search Results. Triggered by keywords. Best for high-intent users ready to buy or take action.
🖼️ Display
Image/text ads across 2M+ websites on the Google Display Network. Great for brand awareness and remarketing.
🛍️ Shopping
Product ads with image, price, and store name. Managed via Google Merchant Center. Ideal for e-commerce.
▶️ Video
Video ads on YouTube and Google video partners. Multiple formats — skippable, non-skippable, bumper, in-feed.
📱 App
Drives app installs and in-app actions across Search, Play, YouTube, and Display. Fully automated by Google AI.
⚡ Performance Max Latest
Single campaign across ALL Google channels. Provide assets + goals; AI handles the rest. Best for high-volume conversions.
💡 Demand Gen
Visual ads on YouTube Home, Gmail, and Discover feeds. Targets users in a discovery mindset. Formerly Discovery Ads.

Search Sub-Types

  • Responsive Search Ads (RSA) — Up to 15 headlines + 4 descriptions; Google AI finds best combinations
  • Dynamic Search Ads (DSA) — Auto-generates ads from website content; great for large catalogs
  • Call-Only Ads — Mobile only; clicking dials your business directly

tCPA — Target Cost Per Acquisition
You set the average cost per lead (e.g., ₹500). Google adjusts bids per auction to hit that target. Lower tCPA = less volume but more efficiency. Higher tCPA = more volume but less efficiency.
tROAS — Target Return on Ad Spend
You set the revenue multiplier (e.g., 400% = ₹4 for every ₹1 spent). Lower tROAS = more scale. Higher tROAS = more profitable but limited volume.
Portfolio Bidding Strategy: A single Smart Bidding strategy applied across multiple campaigns simultaneously — useful for managing budgets at scale.

💡 Pro Tip: Always use Negative Keywords to filter out irrelevant traffic. Check the Search Terms Report weekly to find new negatives.

Other Targeting Methods

  • Location Targeting — Country, city, radius. Set at campaign level.
  • Device Targeting — Desktop, mobile, tablet. Set bid adjustments per device.
  • Dayparting (Ad Schedule) — Show ads only during specific hours/days.
  • Language Targeting — Target users by browser/Google interface language.
  • Demographic Targeting — Age, gender, parental status, household income.
Separate Ad Groups for Competitor vs. Generic Keywords: Always separate them. Competitor keywords need specific "why switch" messaging; generic keywords need broader benefit-driven copy. Mixing them leads to poor Quality Scores and messy data.

Topic 05
Ad Formats
📝 Responsive Search Ads
Up to 15 headlines (30 chars each), 4 descriptions (90 chars). Google AI tests combinations. Headline shows in blue; description below.
🤖 Dynamic Search Ads
Google crawls your site and auto-generates ads. Great for large catalogs and capturing long-tail queries you didn't think of.
🖼️ Responsive Display Ads
Provide headlines, descriptions, logos, images. Google assembles them to fit any ad space on the Display Network.
🛒 Shopping Ads
Image + title + price + store name shown directly in search results. Fed by Google Merchant Center product feed.
Dynamic Keyword Insertion (DKI): Use {KeyWord:Default Text} in headlines. Google auto-inserts the matching keyword into the ad, boosting relevance and CTR. If keyword is too long, the default text is used.

Observation vs. Targeting Mode:
Observation — Shows ads to everyone but lets you track and bid-adjust for specific audiences.
Targeting — Restricts ads only to the selected audience. More focused, smaller reach.
RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads): Use your remarketing lists on Search campaigns. Bid higher for past visitors who are searching your keywords again — they already know you and are far more likely to convert.

Topic 08
Attribution Models

An attribution model decides which ad touchpoint gets credit for a conversion. The customer journey today is multi-step — a user might click a Display ad, then a Generic Search ad, then a Brand Search ad before buying. Which ad gets credit?

ModelCredit AssignmentBest ForLimitation
Last Click100% to last clickBrand/closing keywordsIgnores upper-funnel
First Click (Discontinued)100% to first clickAwareness campaignsIgnores closing actions
Linear (Discontinued)Equal split across all clicksShort, simple journeysDoesn't show high/low value
Time Decay (Discontinued)More credit to recent clicksShort sales cyclesUndervalues early touchpoints
Position-Based (Discontinued)40% first + 40% last + 20% middleBalanced viewFixed rule, not data-driven
Data-Driven (DDA) ⭐ML assigns credit based on real impactBest practice — use thisNeeds conversion volume
✅ Recommendation: Always use Data-Driven Attribution (DDA). It uses machine learning to assign credit based on your specific historical data, giving the most accurate picture of what's actually driving conversions.

Key Tools

  • Keyword Planner — Find new keywords, search volume, and bid estimates
  • Performance Planner — Forecast performance with different budgets
  • Search Terms Report — See actual queries that triggered your ads
  • Auction Insights — Compare your performance vs. competitors
  • Google Ads Editor — Desktop app for bulk offline changes
  • Google Ads Scripts — JavaScript automation for advanced tasks
  • Experiments — A/B test campaigns, bidding strategies, creatives
  • Labels — Tag campaigns/ad groups/keywords for easy filtering & reporting
⚠️ High CTR ≠ Successful Ad. A high CTR only means people are clicking. If clicks don't convert, the ad is wasting budget. Always pair CTR with Conversion Rate, CPA, and ROAS to get the full picture. Common causes of high CTR + low conversions: clickbait copy, wrong landing page, slow page speed, wrong audience.
Enhanced Conversions: Sends hashed (SHA-256) first-party data (email, phone) to Google to recover conversions lost due to cookie restrictions. Improves Smart Bidding accuracy and future-proofs measurement against privacy changes.

Topic 11
Full-Funnel: When to Use Which Format
1
Pre-Launch / Brand Awareness
User is unaware of your product
Use YouTube Non-Skippable + Bumper Ads for guaranteed attention. Use Display (GDN) for visual presence at scale. Target broad Affinity Audiences and Demographics.
2
Consideration
User is researching and comparing
Use Google Search Ads (RSAs) with generic + category keywords. Use In-Feed Video Ads for product demos and tutorials. Layer In-Market Audiences on Search for higher bids.
3
Conversion / Sales
User is ready to buy
Use Shopping Ads for product-specific searches. Use Search Ads with strong CTAs on branded + high-intent long-tail keywords. Use Call-Only Ads for service businesses. Use Lead Form Extensions to reduce friction.
4
Retention / Loyalty
Existing customers
Use Customer Match to target existing customers with exclusive offers. Use Dynamic Remarketing (Display) for up-sells and cross-sells. Use Video Remarketing to stay top-of-mind.

Case Studies
📱
Samsung Phone Launch — ₹70,000 Phone
Total Budget: ₹10,00,000 over 30 days
📅 Pre-Launch (15 days) — ₹3,00,000
Goal: Awareness + pre-order leads
Campaign 1: Performance Max — YouTube teasers, Display images, broad audiences
Campaign 2: Search — Branded + competitor keywords with Lead Form extensions
Bidding: Maximize Conversions + Target CPA (₹200/lead)
📅 Post-Launch (15 days) — ₹7,00,000
Goal: Direct sales & market share
Campaign 1: Performance Max with product feed (Shopping) + Customer Match from pre-launch leads
Campaign 2: Search — Branded, long-tail, review keywords
Bidding: Target ROAS (400%) + Price/Promotion extensions
🏥
Navi Health Insurance Launch
Total Budget: ₹10,00,000 over 30 days | Avg CPC: ₹30–₹70
Pre-Launch — ₹4,00,000
Broad + competitor keywords. Target: "Notify Me" form sign-ups.
Est. ~10,000 clicks @ ₹40 CPC → 500–1,000 leads
Post-Launch — ₹6,00,000
High-intent + branded keywords. Remarketing pre-launch list via Customer Match.
Est. ~12,000 clicks @ ₹50 CPC → 240–600 policy sales
Key Lesson from Both Case Studies: The pre-launch phase builds a warm audience (leads). The post-launch phase converts that warm audience using Customer Match + Performance Max. The two phases work together — never run them in isolation.
